What is space? Space is an almost perfect vacuum, an airless space. Space is not a void: it is permeated with various radiations, and also contains particles of gas, dust and other substances. From Earth, we can observe planets, stars, and galaxies that are located within 46.5 billion light-years in any direction from our planet. This part of the cosmos is called the observable universe. The estimated age of the universe is between 11.4 and 13.8 billion years.

Where does space begin? Space is all that lies beyond the conventional line separating the Earth from outer space. There are different ways to determine exactly where space begins. The most universal reference point is the Karman line, which runs at an altitude of 100 km above mean sea level. Starting from this point, the air becomes too thin for normal aircraft to fly.
